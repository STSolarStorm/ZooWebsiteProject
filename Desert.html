Desert Habitat

Deserts are usually associated with vast expanses of sand dunes and extremely hot temperatures. However, that isn't what all deserts are. According
to National Geographic on deserts, Most experts agree that a desert is an area of land that receives no more than 25 centimeters (10 inches) of precipitation a year.
What does this mean? Well, that means that a place like Antarctica can fall under the category of a desert. So, the world's largest desert actually isn't the Sahara, but
is actually the Antarctic. Shocking, right! A desert isn't just a place that is scorching hot and filed with sand. As long as a place receives minimal quantities of precipitation,
deserts aren't just limited to the common perception.

Gila Monster

Source: Smithsonian's National Zoo & Conservation Biology Institute

The gila monster is a carnivorous venomous lizard that eats small mammals, birds, and eggs. Their metabolism allows them to stay in one place for long periods of time, usually underground,
without needing to hunt for a while.

Camels

These “ships of the desert” have long been valued as pack or saddle animals, and they are also exploited for milk, meat, wool, and hides. The dromedary was domesticated about
3000–2000 BCE in Arabia, the Bactrian camel by 4000 BCE in the steppes of Central Asia. Most of today’s 13 million domesticated dromedaries and roughly 97 domesticated breeds
are in India and in the Horn of Africa.
