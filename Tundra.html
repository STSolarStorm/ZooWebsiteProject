Tundra Habitat

Tundra is the coldest of all the biomes. Tundra comes from the Finnish word tunturi, meaning treeless plain. It is noted for its frost-molded landscapes,
extremely low temperatures, little precipitation, poor nutrients, and short growing seasons. Dead organic material functions as a nutrient pool.

There are two kinds of tundra; Arctic Tundra and Alpine Tundra. The difference is the Arctic Tundra is located in the Arctic while the Alpine Tundra
is located around high altitudes, primarily on mountains

Polar Bear

The largest bear in the world and the Arctic's top predator, polar bears are a powerful symbol of the strength and endurance of the Arctic.

Probably one of the most iconic bears in the world. Famous for their white coat and ferocious power, they are not an animal to be trifled with. There's
even a safety rhyme about them; If the bear is brown, lie down, if it's black, fight back and if it's white, say goodnight

Arctic Fox

If a fox can’t find food, or if the weather gets really bad, it can dig a snow den and hunker down for up to two weeks. As long as a fox is warm, it can
slow down its heart rate and metabolism, which helps the animal save energy so it doesn’t have to eat as much. It’s sort of like how bears hibernate, but
for a shorter period of time.

Arctic Fox's are omnivores that like hunting for small animals. They like to live away from the coasts and have coats that change depending on the season.
