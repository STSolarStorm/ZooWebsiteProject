Ocean Habitat

Massive, vast bodies of water that usually separates large landmasses, like continents. They are full of diverse life forms of plants and aquatic creatures.

Great White Shark

The largest predatory fish in the world – capable of eating marine mammals that weigh several hundred pounds – is the great white shark. The only two fishes that
grow larger than Great Whites are the whale shark and the basking shark, both filter feeders that eat plankton.

When people think of ocean animals, one of the first that comes to mind is most likely a shark, specifically the Great White Shark. Their iconic look
and appearances in movies like Jaws helps to play in that. With rows of serrated teeth that gives them one of the strongest bites in the world,
white underbelly and grey top, their design makes them a deadly predator. Also, they're a delicacy in some Asian countries, being turned into Shark
Fin Soup.

Puffer Fish

In Japan, they are called fugu and are a very expensive, delicious treat. They are prepared only by trained, licensed fugu chefs.

Puffer fish are famous for their unique ability to puff themselves up into a ball.
