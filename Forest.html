Red Fox

Red foxes have excellent hearing. They can hear low-frequency sounds and rodents digging underground.

Red Foxes are small canines with a reddish-orange coat on its back and a white underbelly. They are omnivores with a wide range of foods,
but mainly they are carnivorous, preferring to eat rabbits and small rodents. Red foxes reside on the outskirts of forests, or in open areas
that are within.

Grizzly Bear

After thousands of years traversing western North America, it took barely 100 to push the species close to extinction within the contiguous U.S.
As the conservation movement gathered steam through the 20th century, however, more and more voices called for balance. In 1975, the U.S. Fish and Wildlife
Service listed the grizzly as a threatened species and outlined a plan to help the species recover within its historic range.

Grizzly bears are probably the most famous bear, right beside the polar bear. Known for their ferocity, the grizzly bear is a creature not to be taken lightly
However, as long as they're not threatened or if you're not in their sights at the wrong time of the year, then there should be little to fear. Grizzlies are
omnivores, meaning they have a diverse diet. They can eat things from plants, fruits, and meats. Grizzly bears are famous for their salmon catching.
